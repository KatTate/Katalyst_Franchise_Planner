## 2. Who We Serve (ICP & Qualification Rules)

**Primary ICP:** Privately-owned or private-equity-backed franchisors planning **~10–50 locations per year**, with some corporate stores.

**Category focus:** Service-retail and QSR concepts committed to service excellence and willing to invest in repeatable systems.

**Industries served:** Quick-service and fast-casual restaurants, specialty coffee, retail shops, automotive services, tech repair, outdoor sporting goods, optical care, printing/shipping franchises.

**Qualification rule:** For automated qualification, require two of three indicators:
1. Target openings/year ≥10 and ≤50
2. Willing to fund paid discovery (~$7.5K–$15K)
3. Evidence of franchisee/owner alignment (FDD, existing pilot, or corporate stores)

**Disqualifiers:**
- Publicly-traded brands (unless enterprise approval)
- Brands opening <10 or >50 units/year (unless enterprise approval)
- Concepts unwilling to invest in repeatable systems or paid discovery

## 3. Core Value Propositions

These four value props must appear in short-form templates and proposals. Every outbound proposal or email should contain at least one plus the franchisee-first line unless the output is internal-only.

- **Make scale predictable.** Eliminate permitting, build cost, and standards variability through repeatable playbooks, governance, and controlled execution.
- **Design for all three stakeholders.** The Balanced Servicescape framework designs for Customer Experience (CX), Employee Experience (EX), and Owner Experience (OX) simultaneously so improvements in one area don't break another, preserving sustainable, repeatable unit economics.
- **Vertical integration for execution.** Strategy, interior design, fixture manufacturing, and construction management under one roof reduce the handoffs that cause delays and cost drift. We don't just plan — we build.
- **Franchisee-first philosophy.** Operational tradeoffs are resolved with the North Star: *if franchisees win, we all win.*

## 4. Key Frameworks

When referencing frameworks in proposals, use the exact names and structures below. For short-form content, use the summary line. For SOWs and detailed proposals, use the full layer/dimension breakdowns.

### QuickBrick® Framework
Katalyst's program for opening new locations faster and with more predictability. Each layer must be solid before the next delivers value:

**Layer 1 — Foundation (Chaos Reduction)**
Growth Roadmap, FF&E Plan, Brand Standards, Start-Up Playbook. Gets the basics right so stores can open at all, with consistency.

**Layer 2 — Controlled Costs (Open on Budget)**
Real Estate Strategy, A/E/C Strategy, Servicescape Standards, Construction Project Template. Controls construction costs and ensures predictable bids and budgets.

**Layer 3 — Speed to Open (Open on Time)**
FDD Strategy, Supply Chain Strategy, Service Delivery Blueprint, New Opening Blueprint. Compresses timelines and parallelizes workstreams so stores open faster.

*Summary line for short-form use:* "Foundations → Cost Control → Speed — each layer must be stable before the next delivers value."

### Balanced Servicescape Framework
Methodology for designing service environments that equally consider three stakeholders:
- **CX (Customer Experience):** Welcoming, on-brand, memorable
- **EX (Employee Experience):** Efficient workflow, safe, empowering
- **OX (Owner Experience):** Strong unit economics, profitable, sustainable

Every design decision is evaluated through all three lenses across five dimensions: Environment, Technology, Operations, Training, and Data/Measurement.

### Playbooks & Pilot Governance
Codify the best unit, run governed pilots, measure adoption, then lock standards and training to make the best unit reproducible across the network. Pilot KPIs must be measurable: time to serve, revenue per labor hour, opening schedule adherence, adoption rates.

### Program Delivery Strategies
Three production approaches matched to growth stage — use these when scoping per-location SOWs:
- **Made-to-Order:** Each location's fixtures produced on-demand. Best for early stage or <10 locations/year.
- **Short Run:** Batch production for groups of locations. Best for growth stage, 10–30 locations/year.
- **Material Stocking:** Pre-manufactured components warehoused and ready to ship. Best for high-volume, 30+ locations/year.

### 5-Gear Brand Framework
Maps franchise expansion from Gear 1 (proof-of-concept) to Gear 5 (enterprise scale). Helps brands identify their current growth stage and what they need to focus on next. Key insight: you can't skip gears without risk.

## 5. Productized Offering Path

Use this 4-step path as the default product ladder and the basis for SOW templates. Always default to Foundations as the entry point — only bypass with explicit executive approval.

1. **Fit Check** — Short discovery (free or paid) to establish fit, identify the brand's current gear, and scope next steps.
2. **Katalyst Foundations: Blueprint Session (Hero Offer)** — Paid discovery producing a prioritized roadmap, Conditions of Satisfaction, and Standards Pack. Price: **$7.5K–$15K** (fixed fee).
3. **Katalyst Launch System** — Full rollout strategy and execution partnership. Price band: **$50K–$100K+** (tiered by location count and complexity).
4. **Ongoing Rollout Execution & Production** — Per-location delivery: design, permitting, construction management, fixture manufacturing/fabrication, training, and QA.

## 7. Tone, Positioning & Messaging Guardrails

Enforce these in all generated customer-facing outputs:

- **Voice:** Confident, execution-focused, occasionally witty for short social copy.
- **Position:** "Franchise Scale Partner" — never "vendor," never generic "consultant."
- **North Star:** "We are on the franchisee's side; we exist to help franchisees become wildly successful."
- **Proof over aspiration:** Always back claims with specific outcomes from the Proof Library (Section 11). Numbers > adjectives. If the agent cannot substantiate a claim, use neutral language or flag for human review.
- **Avoid:** Vague aspirational language without operational proof points (e.g., "best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge" with no backing).
